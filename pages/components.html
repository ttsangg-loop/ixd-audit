Components

Components are built up from Building Blocks. They are the organisms found in Atomic Design. Here, we evolve from interactions that focus on state changes, accessing information from overflow menus, etc. and instead,look at interface elements with multiple moving parts and behaviours involves.

Header (Nav Bar)

The navbar is made of a variety of smaller elements, such as tabs, hovering to bring up a dropdown menu, etc.

You can see how hovering over header elements with a downwards caret reveals subpages. The Admin tab does not have a dropdown and you must click into it to see all associated categories (as tabs instead of pages).

On the right side of the navbar, you are able to pull up a search bar, access help links (which can link out to external resources), and user information.

Entry Cards

Our projects and libraries are organized in cards with various bits of information available. These cards not only show as questions to be answered, but also as answered questions that are saved to the Library to be accessed at a later date.

Entry cards can have multiple compliance questions, which are pre-programmed with select choices.

There are a variety of controls hidden in an entry card. Here, you are able to link out the library entry this answer is associated with. The library card looks fairly similar, albeit with reduced capabilities and with a slightly different left panel (search results instead of project contents).

You are also able to create additional answers, bring up a modal to search for relevant entries, and clear answers from this card.

Furthermore, you are able to assign out entries - assignee (someone to answer the question) and reviewer (someone to review it for correctness). From here, you can access a date picker as well as provide additional details (which will appear as a comment).

In the Library, you are able to check for linked entries, comments, pull up an overflow menu, copy answer text, check last review cycle information, see alternative answers, and when/how often this answer is used in projects.

Review Cards

Review Cards are the popups that appear when you click on the circle in the bottom right corner of entry cards. They appear in the Project, Library, and Reviews tab.

You are able to check the freshness of this Library entry that has been assigned to you. Note that the UI of these entry cards (found in the Reviews tab) differs from those found in the Project and Library tabs (e.g. buttons, history, etc... are visually different).

We are not in the library. You are able to see different status colours (e.g. red/grey and yellow) to indicate the freshness of entries.

Modals

We use modals for a variety of reasons: one being to confirm if an action should be done and another one being another screen to allow additional fields to be entered/actions to be done. A modal breaks up the flow on the page and pauses interactions on the main screen before the action has been confirmed or completed.

Here, we are using a modal to create a new project. This is a hidden 2-step modal which requires you to enter information to successfully create a project.

We use modals to send warning and confirmation messages for big moves, such as deleting something.

We use modals in the Project Workspace for a variety of reasons as well, such as rearranging questions, adding entries as additional questions in the Library, etc...

Tables

Tables are used to sort and list information, typically by some sort of chronological or alphabetical order. We employ tables primarily to show progress at a glance, or just to organize lists like users on the platform.

We have hover-oriented pagination where you cannot see how to scroll to the next view of the table until you hover over the available numbers. The table on the home dashboard also lets you hover over elements (e.g. progress bars) to bring up a tooltip on progress. Lastly, you can sort by elements with a caret icon by the header, such as Due Date.

Beyond sorting, you can link out to certain filtered groups of entries. You can also do actions, such as nudging someone (which pulls up a modal) to follow up with their assigned tasks.

We employ tables in the Admin > Users tab. Here, you can sort for users, see their roles and dates created/logged in, and more.
