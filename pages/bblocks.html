Building Blocks

Building blocks are the molecules found in Atomic Design. Here, we look at the interactions, actions, and states of things like buttons, tooltips, dropdowns, etc... in relation to their surroundings. What information do they convey and how?

Buttons

Upon hover, buttons will take on a new state - usually with a defined background or outline and/or a tooltip. If the button is self-explanatory, a tooltip will not accompany it.

Buttons can lead to multiple things: it can lead to a new page, it can open a dropdown, it can open a modal, direct a function (e.g. downloading a file), etc.

Tooltips

We use tooltips on hover to display additional information, such as what figures mean, what links lead to, what buttons and icons indicate, etc...

Tooltips on the platform dashboard - hovering over charts and progress indicators

Tooltips hovering over links

Tooltips in the Projects tab - hovering over buttons and progress indicators. You can see that there is a slight delay from hover to tooltip appearing.

Dropdown

We use dropdowns to hide functions/actions that can be applied to an entry, a way to indicate status changes, and hide additional pages to reduce screen real estate issues for navigation

Dropdowns across the Project List page. These are accompanied with a visible status change for whichever element it is hidden under (e.g. a button, a header tab, etc.)

In the "More Filters" section of the PWS, you can also find a nested dropdown to filter for Compliance Questions.

The Library has an example of a nested dropdown, which then appears as a tag when objects are selected.

Behaviour: Visible/Invisible Elements

To reduce visual clutter, we provide the opportunity for users to hide/open information, such as their answers, potential actions, minimizing/maximizing panels when needed, etc.

On hover, various action become visible in the PWS.

You are able to hide and show answers in the PWS.

In the PWS and Library, you are able to minimize/maximize the side panel to save screen real estate.

Text Entry

Not to be confused with form entry, text entry refers to users editing titles, entries, and comments in the Project Workspace.

This shows the behaviour of editing titles, entry questions (e.g. hover, focus/edit states). This also highlights the behaviour of leaving comments in an entry.

When you choose to edit entry text, you pull up CK Editor and associated interactions/behaviours (e.g. notifying you that you are editing the entry) as well.

Banners

Banners are used across Loopio to confirm actions such as creating or deleting things.

A banner appears to confirm the creation of a new Merge Variable element.

Beyond the modal to confirm the deletion of an element, a banner appears to confirm the action has been completed.

Linking to individual entries

We employ linking to individual entries across the platform - from PWS to library, from integrations to the specific entry, etc... depending on how they got to the single entry, we let users go back to the previous list easily via a back button.
